Color Detector

Here is something interesting where you can implement what you learnt in line-follower!

Components Required

  1. Red LED, Blue LED, Green LED or even a RGB LED would do
  2. OPAMPS
  3. Resistors
  4. Capacitors
  5. MOSFETS
  6. Photodiodes or Photoresistors

Team Mentors

  1. Riddhish Bhalodia

Theory

The working principle behind the color detector is the fact that a Red object will reflect most of the Red light that falls on it, but will absorb the majority of blue and green light that falls on it. So, by determining the intensity of the reflected light that falls on the photodiode/photoresistor, we can get an intuition about the color of the object.

The electric circuitry would involve making a multiplexing circuit for the LEDs and the photodiodes. The circuit would also contain OPAMP based comparators with proper calibration and MOSFETs as electronically controlled switches.

Furthur developments

  • Using Microcontrollers to calculate the exact Red, Green and Blue coefficients of any color
  • Interfacing a 16X2 LCD screen to display the color name and components
  • Make an aid for color blindness
